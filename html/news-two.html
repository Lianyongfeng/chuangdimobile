Relationship between pump head and inlet and outlet water

PAGE VIEW    9999
Pump head and water in and out of the relationship, 8 problems clear, the right choice of pumps!
High head pump for low head pump
Many operators believe that the lower the pumping head, the lower the motor load.Under the misleading of this kind of wrong understanding, when choosing pumps, the pump head is often selected very high.In fact, for centrifugal pumps, when the pump model is determined, its power consumption is proportional to the actual flow of the pump.The flow rate of the pump will decrease with the increase of the head, so the higher the head, the smaller the flow, the smaller the power consumption.Conversely, the lower the head, the greater the flow, the greater the power consumption.Therefore, in order to prevent motor overload, the actual pumping head of water pump should not be less than 60% of the nominal head.Therefore, when the high head is used to pump water at the low head, the motor is liable to overload and heat up.In case of emergency use, a gate valve (or a small outlet plugged with wood, etc.) must be installed in the outlet pipe to adjust the water discharge to reduce the flow and prevent the motor from being overloaded.Pay attention to the temperature rise of the motor. If the motor is found to be overheated, turn down the outlet flow or power off timely.This is also prone to misunderstanding. Some operators believe that blocking the outlet of water, forcing a reduction in the flow rate, will increase the motor load.In fact just the opposite, formal power centrifugal pump irrigation and drainage units are fitted on the outlet pipe of gate valve, in order to reduce the unit startup motor load, should be closed gate, after waiting for motor start and gradually open the gate.
